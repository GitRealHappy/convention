The Future-Proof Transformation Contest

Draft proposal — feedback welcome

I'm exploring a contest alongside the 8-week workshop series: free workshops for everyone, ticket required to enter the contest, speakers vote on one winner. I'm not sure the structure is right or if it's too complicated — I'd welcome your thoughts.

Contest Overview

Expand any section for details. I'd welcome your take on whether this is too much, or if you'd suggest doing it differently.

The Concept

The contest would run alongside the 8-week Future-Proof Workshop Series (leading to the June 5–7 summit). The workshop series itself is free — anyone can attend any of the live calls at no cost. To enter the contest, participants would need a GA or VIP ticket to the summit and would post after each workshop. The contest would reward the one who demonstrates the most visible transformation across all 8 weeks.

Contestants would post a reel on Instagram or a short on YouTube after each workshop, describing how they're applying what they learned. No application form — eligibility would be verified through tagged posts. At the end of the series, the workshop speakers would review the eligible entries and vote on a single winner. This is a working draft; I'm open to simplifying if it feels like too much.

The winner is announced on May 2nd, one week after the final workshop (not on stage at the convention).

The Prize

If the winner has a GA ticket:

  • Free upgrade to VIP ($700 value — the difference between GA at $299 and VIP at $999)

If the winner already has a VIP ticket:

  • A free VIP ticket to bring a friend ($999 value). Their friend gets the full VIP experience alongside them.

In both cases, the prize is worth $700–$999 USD and deepens the winner's connection to the summit community.

VIP includes: exclusive Saturday dinner with speakers, front-row seating, after-party ticket, and 2 years of Premium Living Internet University membership.

How Participants Enter

Anyone can join the workshop series for free and attend any live call at no cost. To enter the contest, participants must:

  1. Hold a GA or VIP ticket to the Future-Proof Creator Summit (June 5–7, 2026).
  2. Attend each of the 8 workshops — either live or by watching the recorded replay.
  3. Post a reel on Instagram or a video on YouTube after each workshop, describing how they are applying what they learned in their own life or business.
  4. Tag #livinginternetuniversity and @living.internet.alliance in every post.
  5. Post for all 8 workshops — missing even one disqualifies the entry.

Eligibility would be verified through the tagged posts. I might also ask participants to submit a simple form at the end with links to all 8 posts to make speaker review easier.

How the Winner Is Selected

At the end of the 8-week series, the workshop speakers would review the eligible entries and vote on one winner based on a single criterion:

Most visible transformation across the 8 weeks.

I'd be looking for:

  • Genuine, honest documentation of their journey (not polished production)
  • Real-world application of what they learned — not just summaries
  • Evidence of growth, breakthroughs, and honest struggle
  • A story arc that shows who they were in week 1 vs. who they are in week 8

Tie-breaking: If speakers are split, the series host casts the deciding vote.

The winner is announced on May 2nd, one week after the final workshop (not on stage at the convention).

What I'm Asking of You

At the end of the 8-week series (Week 9), I'd send you the eligible entries — likely a shortlist with links to each participant's 8 posts. One idea is that you'd review them and cast a single vote for the participant who, in your view, showed the most visible transformation across the 8 weeks.

I'm not looking for the most polished content — I'm looking for the most honest journey: real application of what was taught, evidence of growth, and a clear story arc from week 1 to week 8.

I might also give 2nd and 3rd place a shout-out at the summit (with runner-up prize) to reward effort; your votes would help identify those finalists.

I'd welcome your feedback on whether this structure works or if it's too much to ask.
