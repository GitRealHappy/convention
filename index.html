The attention economy is almost over.

Right now, in early 2026, there is a short window open where those who act will gain first-mover advantage in the next stage of the Digital Renaissance.

The winners over the next few decades will be the polymaths and generalists who learn fast, adapt, and invent new patterns and systems to help people navigate through the world.

The idea that being "creative" means that you'll be a struggling artist is coming to an end.

Human creativity is the foundation of the modern world.

True creators are those who have innovated all of the systems and technology that define our lives today. The ones who created the wheel, and eventually the car. Engineered planes and electricity. Brought us to the moon. Architected the computer, internet, and communication technology. Great people like Aristotle, Leonardo Da Vinci, Galileo, Nikola Tesla, who redefined how humans live in the world.

And the internet has decentralized the opportunity to step into their shoes. In the past you would have needed a tremendous amount of wealth, status, education, and training. But today, you only need to direct your focus precisely, and you can unlock the doors of your choice.

Most of us have tunnel-vision when it comes to the real opportunity that the internet represents.

  • To some, they see a way to replace their current career or build one from scratch.
  • To some it is an alternative path to college or university.
  • To some it is a way to make an extra couple hundred, or couple thousand dollars per month to augment their income.
  • To some it represents a potential path to wealth, status, and financial freedom.

These are all real opportunities, and many people find success with them. But for a select few who dive deeper, they are only pieces of the puzzle.

For a new generation of philosophers, thinkers, and decentralized educators these platforms are a means to an impact. They want to equip others with diversified skills, agency, and potency in the modern world.

The trend of inauthentic content, overpromising, and incomplete perspectives will fall flat to the future-proof skillstack that has defined every revolution in human history.

The Future-Proof Creator Summit has been created to give you all of the pieces to the puzzle.

How to…

  • Learn and teach first principles to develop expertise fast.
  • Build real relationships, which are the basis of sales, business deals, and collaborations.
  • Develop agency to take permissionless action, staying true to yourself regardless of external pressures.
  • Make your body and mind healthy to boost the quality of what you create.
  • Write for impact and persuasion.
  • Build an audience who trusts you.
  • Bring your authentic self to your content.
  • Create offers and test them.
  • Get leads.
  • Sell and market your products or services.
  • Use AI to multiply your productivity and/or automate your work.

The harsh truth is that everything mentioned above is a skill that overlaps with all of the other skills. So it is
1) Extremely challenging to learn them in isolation and
2) Almost impossible to embody them without a real group of people pursuing the same goals.

We want to gather people who share this perspective and empower them to use it to maximum effect.

You've Got Talent and Knowledge. You Have Something to Offer. But...

  • You hit publish, refresh the page, and... nothing. Again. The algorithm doesn't care how long that took you, and the silence is starting to feel personal.
  • The people in your life smile and nod, but you can feel it — they're waiting for you to "get a real job." You don't have a single person in your corner who actually gets what you're building.
  • You have a nagging voice that says: "Who am I to charge for this?" You've rewritten your offer page six times and still haven't hit publish, because what if nobody buys?
  • You scroll past another creator celebrating their $50K month and your chest tightens. They started after you. What do they know that you don't?
  • You've actually built something that works — but you're duct-taping it together at midnight, running on caffeine and willpower, and you know this pace will break you.

This is where that changes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is this event for?

The Future-Proof Creator Summit is for creators of all kinds: writers, artists, filmmakers, podcasters, youtubers, coaches, freelancers, entrepreneurs. Anyone building their work online and ready to take it to the next level.

Where will this event be held?

The event will be held at the Sheraton Vancouver Airport Hotel. Full details — maps, transportation tips, and local recommendations — will be sent to ticket holders and shared in our private Circle community. For its guests, the hotel offers a free shuttle from YVR, a 24-hr gym, and a heated pool.

How long is the event and what’s the schedule like?

It’s a 3-day event starting Friday, June 5 at 1:00PM and ending Sunday, June 7 at 4:30PM.

We’ll also have community virtual meetups before and after the weekend to keep momentum strong. A full schedule will be released closer to the date. Plan for inspiring days punctuated by short breaks for rest and conversation.

The after-party will be held on Sunday night at 8:00PM at a private venue.

Are tickets to the Sunday after-party included?

After-party tickets are sold separately (but are included with VIP tickets). They are $80 USD. Access to after-party tickets will be granted through your welcome email.

Are there nearby hotels?

Yes! We’ve arranged special group rates at the Sheraton (the host hotel) and the Marriott right next door, so you can stay close to the action and connect outside of sessions. Details will be sent in your welcome email. You’re encouraged to coordinate in the community to share rooms and save costs.

How much do hotel rooms cost?

Rooms at the Sheraton and Marriot, for event attendees, are $399/night in Canadian Dollars, which is around $290 USD. These rooms can officially fit up to 4 people, so if you'd like to save on costs we suggest attending with people you know, or joining the Living Internet community early and start connecting.

What is the ticket refund policy?

Tickets are non-refundable. They are transferable until May 1st, 2026.

Will we be able to meet the speakers?

Many speakers include time for questions during or after their talks, and may mingle before/after sessions. VIP ticket-holders will also have access to an intimate Saturday dinner with speakers and fellow creators.
